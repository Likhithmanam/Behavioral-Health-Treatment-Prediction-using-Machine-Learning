Mental health conditions affect an estimated one in four working-age adults globally, yet most individuals experiencing significant psychological distress never seek professional treatment. Stigma, limited awareness of employer-sponsored resources, and social barriers are the primary reasons. Left unaddressed, untreated mental health conditions reduce productivity, increase absenteeism, and drive significant healthcare costs for organizations.
This project addresses a direct business problem: given observable behavioral and demographic survey data, can we predict whether an individual is likely to seek mental health treatment? Using 225,058 self-reported survey records collected from 2022 to 2024, we build and compare machine learning classification models capable of generating individual risk scores from behavioral and workplace indicators. The primary objective is binary classification of treatment-seeking behavior (Yes/No). As a secondary analysis, we also model mood swing severity — a variable indirectly related to the primary treatment outcome — across seven models using a 70/30 split to provide a complementary emotional risk profile.
